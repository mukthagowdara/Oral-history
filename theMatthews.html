The Matthews

Ardelle Matthews


Early Years

Born in town called Arkadelphia, Arkansas and moved with her mother and three siblings to Cotton plant, Arkansas. She attended the Arkadelphia-CottonPlant academy, which was a presbyterian mission school and a close-knit community. Here she also learned to play the piano. Her aunt visited her mother when Ardelle was 6 years old and she moved with her to Philadelphia for 5 years and that is where she started education. She remembers her teachers and friends from the academy – Dorothy Smith, Reverend Dwight. Then she had the opportunity to go to college on a presbyterian scholarship – Emporia, Kansas where there were just 4-5 African American students. There she met her husband Robert Matthews and were married in 1952. They have 3 children - Mark, Ryan and Scott. After her husband came out of the military, they moved to Philadelphia in 1954 and she describes it as a wonderful time to live in Philadelphia with all the cultural experiences of museums, orchestra, plays and more.

Move to San Diego and community work

In 1956, they moved to San Diego and this is when San Diego was growing and there was demand for teachers. They joined the presbyterian church called the Golden Hue where Reverend Smith was the founder. She joined the choir. At that time the church merged with Brooklyn Heights presbyterian church and became Christ United presbyterian church and they have been there ever since. She has been involved with presbyterian women and assisted with anything necessary for the church, been an elder, and also worked as a president of the choir.

Mrs. Ardelle Matthews has taught at Sherman Elementary school for 14 years between 1964 - 1996. The specialty there was that parents had tours and were free to come onto classes anytime. Then she moved to San Diego school of Creative and Performing Arts before retiring.

She is a member of the Neighborhood counsel. She firmly believes in “being proactive for the things that her community needs”. The neighborhood counsel meets once a month at the Malcolm X library. She is a member of the Rays children’s hospital auxilary and raises money for the hospital. They also adopt, provide and take care of a child who is incapacitated in the hospital. The hospital has 32 units all over San Diego and there are many such children in need of providers.



Dr. Robert Matthews


Early Years

Born in a small town in Kansas called Tonganoxie which is approximately 17 miles west of Kansas City Missouri and was a disputed territory during the Civil war. He went to school in a situation where he did not have a black male companion in the same grade level until he was at the University of Kansas. Back then a person of color couldn’t take a book home from the library. His mother worked as a domestic help in white household kitchen and they supplied him with books. In 2010, his school started the Hall of Fame for achievers and the very first name was Dr. Robert Matthews.

Professional Life

Dr Matthews began his career as a teacher in Kansas City. He joined the Army and was first stationed at Arkansas. He was trained as an artillery specialist because he had a design background. He was sent to the Armed Force Institution in New York and he also wrote a few papers for the Armed Forces Army Times which led him to Post Information Educational Headquarters, Louisiana. He then decided to go to Columbia University for a Master’s degree in Child Education and graduated in 1955. He enrolled in Temple University, Philadelphia for his Doctorate. He and his family then moved to San Diego.

San Diego and community work

Tema Ghana Sister City – Dr. Matthews is the President of this African sister city and Mrs. Ardelle is the Secretary. They try to develop and provide for African nations by providing fund for the creation of clean water for African children through this sister city. They support Dr. Butcher who goes every other year and builds a well or maintains a built well for clean water. And also training people in tactical engineering advice there to build roads and other facilities. They also gather generic medicines and send them to Ghana. They would create an African marketplace on Sunday afternoons at Educational Cultural Complex by gathering items authentic to Africa like cookbooks of authentic dishes and sell them to raise funds.

Bob and Ardelle have respectively chaired the San Diego Annual Martin Luther King, Jr. Parade and the MLK, Jr. King and Queen pageant. King and Queen Pageant was conducted in November and the parade was in January.

Interesting snippets

He started working in farm as a young boy helping his grandfather by picking corns, picking potatoes, etc. In 1940s when they were working away in the potato field, a convoy of army trucks came onto the field and they were told that they were to not have anything to do with these individuals. They all had beautiful men with blonde hair. These men were German prisoners of war from world war II. They were assigned to help pick up potatoes and help out. They became friends and in the late 70s when Dr. Matthews joined the urban league in San Diego, they had a contract with Air Force in Colorado springs to recruit young men from San Diego for Air Force. One of the men who came to meet Dr. Matthews was one of the prisoners of war as a young boy. He had become a citizen and joined the Air Force.
